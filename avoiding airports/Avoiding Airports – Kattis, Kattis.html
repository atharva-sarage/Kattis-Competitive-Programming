Avoiding Airports

David is looking to book some travel over the world. There are nn countries that he can visit, and mm flights that are available. The iith flight goes from country aiai to country bibi. It departs at time sisi, and lands at time eiei.

David is currently at the airport in country 11, and the current time is 00, and he would like to travel country nn. He does not care about the total amount of time needed to travel, but he really hates waiting in the airport. If he waits tt seconds in an airport, he gains t2t2 units of frustration. Help him find an itinerary that minimizes the sum of frustration.

A flight might have the same departure and arrival country.

No two flights will have the same arrival time, or have the same departure time. In addition, no flight will have the same arrival time as the departure time of another flight. Finally, it is guaranteed that there will always be a way for David to arrive at his destination.

Output

Print, on a single line, the minimum sum of frustration.

Examples

In the first sample, it is optimal to take this sequence of flights:

  • Flight 55. Goes from airport 11 to airport 22, departing at time 33, arriving at time 88.

  • Flight 33. Goes from airport 22 to airport 11, departing at time 99, arriving at time 1212.

  • Flight 77. Goes from airport 11 to airport 33, departing at time 1313, arriving at time 2727.

  • Flight 88. Goes from airport 33 to airport 55, deparing at time 2828, arriving at time 100100.

Note that there is an itinerary that gets David to his destination faster. However, that itinerary has a higher total frustration.
